Emma Bowen Foundation Fellow at WBAL-TV in Baltimore

Worked in rotation through several different departments in the newsroom including: reporters, producers, the assignment desk, photographers, the digital, sales and creative services departments. Gained and strengthened various skills through my work, including: futures planning, producing a newscast, field reporting, interviewing, anchoring, writing for broadcast and the web, promotions writing, and knowledge of the advertising and sales sector of the industry. Also acquired overall knowledge in how a newsroom runs, what constitutes news in a certain area, and how the relationship between a news organization and the society it serves affects each other.

Dow Jones News Fund Digital Media Program

Attended a 9-day intensive training in which I gained skills in analyzing web traffic and social media, learning the latest visual and audio storytelling techniques-preparing me for the rapidly evolving media ecosystem, taught by faculty from the Walter Cronkite School of Journalism at Arizona State University and other professional journalists. As a culmination of our training, we also researched, reported and produced stories about the recovery of their local communities and businesses, producing the website, Back in Business, in which I was a web developer. The website is a multimedia package of videos, audio, photos, infographics and original reporting.

Medill- Northwestern Journalism Institute Cherub

A selective five-week journalism program. Learned to report in various styles using different types of media including, print, photography, broadcast and digital. Gained vast experience in field reporting, photography, interviewing and many multimedia platforms, with various resources in the field.

Mentorship at WBAL-TV 11 News

Mentored and worked closely with WBAL's anchors Jason Newton and Mindy Basara as well as meteorologist Ava Marie. Learned how to write news stories for broadcast tv, record voiceovers, put together news packages and introduced to the workings of meteorology. Also, learned the workings of a news station.

Urban Health Media Project Fellow (Kellogg Foundation and USA Today)

Taught to report on health and social issues that are often overlooked in their communities, using multi-media techniques. Gained experience that expanded my knowledge of the industry as well as developed active reporting skills. Published an article, “Parents may fret, but teens and even experts say social media use has its benefits”, March 2018 in USA Today.
